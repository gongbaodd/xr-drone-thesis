\section*{\centering Abstract}
\noindent 
Automation is progressively displacing skilled technical labor, however, current robotic systems remain limited in replicating complex tasks, leaving a persistent need for human operators. At the same time, such skills are difficult to acquire, requiring prolonged training and the development of muscle memory. This results in sustained demand for skilled operators, while relatively few individuals are willing to pursue training.

\noindent
This paper presents a design-based research (DBR) study investigating Extended Reality (XR) as a medium for lowering the learning barrier and increasing engagement in complex skill acquisition, using First Person View (FPV) quadcopter piloting as a representative case domain.

\noindent
This study is structured around three primary objectives: (1) the simulation of FPV quadcopter flight physics, (2) the design of a human-AI collaboration agent, and (3) the development of interaction techniques for high-fidelity XR prototyping. In particular, experiential learning is used to structure learning guidance, PID control and reinforcement learning (RL) are applied in the human-AI collaboration agent, and geographic data are integrated to support environmental representation.

\noindent
The contribution of this work is a structured design framework that links physical simulation, human-AI collaboration, and XR-based interaction design, aimed at reducing the learning curve and improving accessibility to complex skill training.
